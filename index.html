Why We Care about Students

What is significant about Kindergarten - 12th grade (ages 5-18)?

The Washington State Constitution says the state has a paramount duty to educate children.

"It is the paramount duty of the state to make ample provision for the education of all children residing within its borders, without distinction or preference on account of race, color, caste, or sex."

Washington Constitution, article IX, section 1

What we know

How many full-time students are there?

##    Min. 1st Qu.  Median    Mean 3rd Qu.    Max. 
##     9.1   252.0   940.5  3399.0  3444.0 47570.0

In 2012-2013 there were 1002848 students. They spanned 39 counties and 295 school districts.

There was an extreme distribution of students throughout the state. The mean number of students per school district was 3399, but the largest school district had 47570 students, and the smallest school district had 9 students.

How this Application Helps

What can this application help me understand?

It graphically illustrates the scope of student enrollment throughout the state. You can explore individual school district enrollment by county and compare county figures to statewide figures.

The Future

I want to learn more. Where do I start?

Visit the Office of Superintendent of Public Instruction. This state office is the custodian of enrollment and financial information for K-12 education.
